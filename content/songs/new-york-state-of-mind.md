There are songs that describe places, and there are songs that describe how a place feels when it is yours. This is the second kind.

Billy Joel wrote this on a bus back to New York after a stretch of time away. No grand occasion. Just the familiar pull of somewhere that fits you. The piano comes in slow, almost reluctant, and then it settles — exactly like arriving home.

I put this on the list because I want you to know that feeling. The feeling of somewhere being yours not because you were born there, but because something in you chose it. That is a rare thing, and worth holding onto.
