Paul McCartney said he dreamed of his mother, who had died when he was fourteen. In the dream she came to him and told him it would be alright. Let it be.

That is the entire song. A dream. A mother's voice. The permission to stop struggling.

I find it almost impossible to explain why this song hits so hard without explaining that sometimes the most radical thing you can do is accept something. Not give up — accept. Let the thing be what it is.
